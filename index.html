    • Types OF Cyber Security Attacks

    A computer virus is a type of malicious software (malware) designed to replicate and spread from one computer to another. It can attach itself to legitimate programs or files, causing damage to the host system or disrupting its normal functioning.The goals of computer viruses vary and may include causing harm, stealing information, or providing unauthorized access to a computer system. To protect against computer viruses, users often use antivirus software and adopt good cybersecurity practices, such as keeping software up to date and being cautious with email attachments and downloads
    SQL injection is a type of cyber attack where malicious SQL (Structured Query Language) code is inserted into input fields or parameters of a web application. The goal is to manipulate the application's database by exploiting vulnerabilities in its code. This can lead to unauthorized access, data manipulation, or even the deletion of data. Proper input validation and the use of parameterized queries are essential defenses against SQL injection attacks.
    Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) is a type of web security vulnerability where attackers inject malicious scripts into web pages that are then viewed by other users. These scripts can execute in the context of the victim's browser, potentially allowing the attacker to steal sensitive information, manipulate content, or perform actions on behalf of the user. Prevention involves input validation, proper encoding, and implementing security mechanisms like Content Security Policy (CSP).
    A Denial-of-Service (DoS) attack or its more potent variant, Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack, aims to disrupt the normal functioning of a target's network or online service. In a DoS attack, a single source overwhelms the target with a flood of traffic, causing service unavailability. DDoS attacks involve multiple sources, often coordinated through a botnet, amplifying the impact. The goal is to exhaust the target's resources, bandwidth, or processing power, rendering it inaccessible to legitimate users. Mitigation strategies include traffic filtering, load balancing, and the use of specialized DDoS protection services.

    Cyber Security Attacks

    August 2023: Russian hacktivists launched DDoS attacks against Czech banks and the Czech stock exchange. The hackers cut online banking access to the banks clients and demanded that the institutions stop supporting Ukraine. Bank representatives claim the hacks did not threaten their clients finances.


    September 2023: Indian hacktivists targeted Canada military and Parliament websites with DDoS attacks that slowed system operations for several hours. Hacktivists referenced Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau public accusation against India of killing Sikh independence activist Hardeep Singh Nijjar as motivation for the hack.


    Octomber 2023: Pro-Hamas and pro-Israeli hacktivists have launched multiple cyberattacks against Israeli government sites and Hamas web pages in the aftermath of Hamas attacks on Israel on October 7th. Russian and Iranian hacktivists also targeted Israeli government sites, and Indian hacktivists have attacked Hamas websites in support of Israel.
